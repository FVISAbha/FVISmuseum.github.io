Natural Environment Section

The wildlife of Saudi Arabia is substantial and varied. Saudi Arabia is a very large country forming the biggest part of the Arabian Peninsula. It has several geographic regions, each with a diversity of plants and animals adapted to their own particular habitats.

The Oasis is part of the AlUla Old Town Villageز Ancient civilisations have cultivated this spring-fed oasis for millennia, and today it remains a lush and fertile plain with sustainable farming at its core.

The dromedary, also known as the dromedary camel, Arabian camel, or one-humped camel, is a large even-toed ungulate, of the genus Camelus, with one hump on its back. It is the tallest of the three species of camel; adult males stand 2.2–3.6 m at the shoulder, while females are 1.7–1.9 m tall.

One notable species of eagles in Saudi Arabia is the Arabian eagle, also known as the Short-toed Snake Eagle (Circaetus gallicus). This bird of prey is found in arid regions, including Saudi Arabia, and is known for its distinctive appearance and feeding habits, primarily preying on snakes.

Saudi Arabia is home to various snake species, including venomous ones like vipers and cobras. Common species include the Arabian cobra and saw-scaled viper. While encounters with snakes are possible, many are not aggressive unless provoked. It's advised to be cautious in desert and rocky areas where snakes may reside.

The Arabian leopard is the smallest subspecies of leopard, and is highly endangered. It can be found in the mountains along the west coast, and has been seen in the Jabal Shadah Reserve.

Saudi Arabia is home to various species of monkeys, particularly in the southwestern regions around the Asir Province and the mountainous areas. The most notable species is the Arabian baboon (Papio hamadryas arabicus), which is a subspecies of the hamadryas baboon. These baboons are adapted to arid environments and are known to inhabit rocky areas. .
