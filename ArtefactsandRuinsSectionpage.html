Artefacts and Ruins Section

Explore captivating exhibits featuring artifacts, historical narratives, and multimedia presentations meticulously curated to showcase the rich tapestry of Saudi Arabia's cultural heritage.

Similar to Petra in Jordan, Hegra features well-preserved rock-cut tombs and other structures, showcasing the unique Nabataean architectural style.The site provides valuable insights into the ancient Arabian civilizations and their sophisticated building techniques.

Hegra, also known as Al-Hijr or Madain Saleh, is a UNESCO World Heritage site in Saudi Arabia. It's an archaeological site located in the Al Ula area and was once part of the Nabataean Kingdom. Hegra is a significant cultural and historical treasure in Saudi Arabia.

Al Ula, located in northwestern Saudi Arabia, is renowned for its UNESCO World Heritage Site, Madain Saleh. This archaeological site features well-preserved Nabataean tombs and rock-cut facades, similar to those in Petra, Jordan. The intricate carvings and ancient inscriptions provide a glimpse into the Nabataean civilization.

Al Ula also boasts a stunning landscape with towering sandstone mountains, ancient mud-brick forts, and oases. The city has a history dating back thousands of years, with evidence of various civilizations that once thrived in the region. In recent years, Saudi Arabia has opened Al Ula to tourism, allowing visitors to experience its unique blend of history, culture, and natural beauty.
