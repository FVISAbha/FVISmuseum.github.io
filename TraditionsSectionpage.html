Traditions Section

From ancient traditions to modern innovations, this digital museum offers a dynamic and educational experience, preserving and celebrating the essence of Saudi national identity for global audiences.

Bedouin weaving is a traditional craft practiced by the Bedouin people, nomadic desert dwellers with a rich cultural heritage. This intricate form of weaving involves creating textiles using traditional techniques passed down through generations.

Al Arda is a lively and expressive traditional dance in Saudi Arabia, often performed during festive events, weddings, and national celebrations. It involves a group of male participants who form two rows, facing each other. The dancers, dressed in traditional attire, move in synchronized steps and gestures.

The Ramadan markets in Saudi Arabia are vibrant hubs of cultural exchange, offering traditional crafts, antiques, and folk items. These markets often set up during the Holy Month, showcase the rich heritage and artistic skills of the region, providing a unique shopping experience for locals and visitors alike.

Saudi crafting skills include intricate hand-woven textiles, detailed metalwork, traditional pottery, Arabic calligraphy, wooden items with ornate carvings, unique jewelry, and palm leaf weaving by Bedouin communities. These crafts showcase the country's rich cultural heritage and skilled artisans..
